Question-1: What are the different ways to select an element in the DOM?

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

Here are the different ways to select an element in the DOM :

1. document.getElementById("id")
2. document.getElementsByClassName("class")
3. document.getElementsByTagName("tag")
4. document.querySelector("selector")
5. document.querySelectorAll("selector")

Question-2: What is the difference between innerHTML, innerText, and textContent ?

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

Here are the differences between innerHTML, innerText and textContent :

innerHTML : Using that we can access all the content of an element including html tags.
innerText : Using that we can also access the contents but in form of text. Basically we use this if element is not an input element.
textContent : Using that we can access all the text value from the element whatever it visible or not.

Question-4: What is event bubbling in the DOM?

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

Event bubbling : This process works like an actual bubble. In this process event moves from down to up. We can say like an tree also.
In this process events happens from child to move to up parent and then move to up grandparent and so on.
